## Email 2: Formal proposal (the attachment to forward)

---

**Hong Kong Clubs Backgammon League**
*A proposal for inter-club competition*

**The concept**

We are establishing a backgammon league between Hong Kong's leading private members' clubs, modelled on the London Clubs Backgammon League. The London league has run successfully since 2005 between clubs including the Royal Automobile Club, Hurlingham Club, MCC, Chelsea Arts Club, Roehampton Club, and others. It is widely regarded as one of the most enjoyable inter-club sporting fixtures on the London calendar.

We would like to invite [Club Name] to participate in the inaugural Hong Kong season.

**How it works**

- Each club fields a team of 4 players (expanding to 6 as the league grows)
- Teams meet once a month at the host club, September through May
- Each player plays three short matches (5-point) against their opposite number
- The home club hosts dinner and drinks for all players — typically 8-12 people
- Fixtures alternate home and away each season
- Results are recorded on a dedicated league website with automatic standings

A typical evening runs from 7pm to 10:30pm, with a supper break between rounds.

**What the club provides**

- A private or semi-private room one evening per month (when hosting)
- Dinner and drinks for visiting players (at members' or club rates)
- That's it. We provide all backgammon equipment and handle all league administration.

**Why it works for clubs**

- **Revenue**: Each home fixture generates dinner and bar spend for 8-12 guests
- **Networking**: Inter-club competition creates connections between members of Hong Kong's most prestigious clubs
- **Low overhead**: No dedicated facilities needed — just a room with tables
- **Tradition**: Backgammon sits alongside chess, bridge, and mahjong in the tradition of gentlemen's club games. Many London clubs now consider their backgammon team a point of pride
- **Social**: The London league is described by its participants as "first and foremost about social backgammon, having fun, making friends, and experiencing hospitality at other clubs"

**Pedigree**

The league operates under the auspices of the Hong Kong Backgammon Federation, affiliated with the World Backgammon Federation (WBGF). Our rules are based on those of the UK Backgammon Federation, the same framework used by the London league.

The London Clubs Backgammon League is coordinated by Tony Lezard, a board member of the UK Backgammon Federation, who is supporting the establishment of this Hong Kong equivalent and will host the league website.

**No experience needed**

Clubs do not need existing backgammon players to participate. We offer free introductory sessions at your club to teach interested members. Backgammon takes five minutes to learn and the strategic depth keeps people playing for a lifetime. We find that members who play chess, bridge, or mahjong pick it up immediately.
