# Hong Kong Backgammon Federation & Inter-Club League — Strategic Plan

## Context

Setting up two things upon relocating to Hong Kong:
1. **The Hong Kong Backgammon Federation (HKBF)** — a governing body for competitive backgammon in HK
2. **The Hong Kong Clubs Backgammon League** — an inter-club league modelled on the [London Clubs Backgammon League](https://www.londonbackgammonleague.com/about) run by Tony Lezard

The existing [Hong Kong Backgammon Club](https://www.meetup.com/hong-kong-backgammon-club/) has ~250 registered members but the weekly meetup draws only 5-10 people at a rundown pub. The scene is small and informal. A key motivation for the inter-club league is to play regularly in much nicer settings, and to grow the community from this low base.

## Part 1: Hong Kong Clubs Backgammon League

### Format (adapted from London model)

The London League uses: **6 players per team, each playing 3x 5-point matches = 18 match points per fixture**. The evening includes supper and drinks hosted by the home club. Fixtures alternate home/away each season.

**Proposed HK format:**

| Element | Proposal | Notes |
|---------|----------|-------|
| Team size | 4 players (Season 1), scaling to 6 | Start smaller given HK's thin player base; grow to London's 6-a-side as squads deepen |
| Match format | 3x 5-point matches per player | 12 points per fixture at 4-a-side (18 at full 6-a-side); keeps evenings to ~2.5-3 hours |
| Doubling cube | Yes (UKBGF rules) | Standard competitive play |
| Clocks | Optional, by agreement of both captains | As per London rules |
| Season structure | Round-robin, one fixture per month | Sep-May (~8 months, one match per opposing club) |
| Home/away | Alternating each season | Home club hosts dinner + provides boards |
| Scoring | Match points won across all games | Tiebreaker: head-to-head, then individual match point differential |

### Target Clubs (5 for inaugural season)

| Club | Location | Why |
|------|----------|-----|
| **Ladies' Recreation Club (LRC)** | Mid-Levels | 5,000 members, strong social scene, good facilities |
| **The American Club** | Central / Tai Tam | Two clubhouses, strong expat community |
| **Hong Kong Cricket Club (HKCC)** | Wong Nai Chung Gap | Historic (est. 1851), already hosts competitive sports leagues |
| **Royal HK Yacht Club (RHKYC)** | Causeway Bay / Repulse Bay / Sai Kung | Prestigious, 3 locations, social dining culture |
| **Hong Kong Football Club (HKFC)** | Happy Valley | Strong inter-club sports tradition, snooker/bowls culture |

### Expansion targets (Season 2+)
- Hong Kong Club (Central) — the most exclusive, major credibility signal
- Foreign Correspondents' Club (FCC) — media/social hub
- Aberdeen Marina Club — Shangri-La managed, south side
- Kowloon Cricket Club — extends reach to Kowloon
- China Club — David Tang legacy, arts/business crowd

### Club Recruitment Approach

Starting from scratch on club connections. Strategy: approach as many plausible clubs as possible; Season 1 includes whoever responds. **Minimum viable league = 3 clubs.**

**Outreach strategy:**

1. **Write to club General Managers / Activities Coordinators** — most HK private clubs have a dedicated social/activities team. Send a formal one-page proposal positioning the league as a new inter-club social competition
2. **Leverage Tony Lezard as a reference** — "Modelled on the London Clubs Backgammon League, running successfully for 20+ years between the RAC, Hurlingham, MCC, and other prestigious London clubs." Ask Tony for an endorsement letter
3. **Leverage WBGF affiliation** — even "in progress" affiliation with the World Backgammon Federation adds credibility
4. **The pitch to clubs** — frame it as low-cost, high-social-value:
   - Generates bar/dining revenue on match nights (dinner + drinks for 8-12 people)
   - Creates inter-club networking (appeals to corporate members)
   - Requires minimal facilities (a private room + 4 boards)
   - Aligns with gentlemen's games tradition (chess, bridge, mahjong already at most clubs)
   - Proven 20-year track record in London's equivalent clubs
5. **Offer free taster sessions** at each club — teach interested members, play casual games, gauge interest
6. **Recruit within clubs** — target chess/bridge/mahjong players as the natural crossover audience. The venue quality is the hook, not "join the backgammon community"
7. **Use the HK BG Club meetup as a training ground** — invite club members to learn/practice before league play

**Proposal letter should include:**
- One-page league concept summary
- London League as precedent (with photos if possible)
- Minimal club requirements (a room, one evening per month)
- Offer to provide all equipment
- Reference from Tony Lezard / UKBGF

### Match Night Format (following London model)

```
7:00 PM  — Players arrive, welcome drinks
7:30 PM  — Round 1 (5-point matches, ~30 min each)
8:15 PM  — Supper break (hosted by home club)
9:00 PM  — Round 2
9:45 PM  — Round 3
10:30 PM — Results, socialising
```

### Equipment & Logistics
- Each home club provides boards, dice, shakers, cubes
- **Startup option**: Federation purchases 4-6 quality tournament sets that travel with the away team if a club doesn't own boards

### Website & Results
- **Tony Lezard to host the league website**, modelled on the London League site (londonbackgammonleague.com)
- Electronic result entry by captains after each fixture (same system as London)
- Automatic standings, match history, and player statistics
- This removes the need to build anything custom — proven platform, maintained by Tony

## Part 2: Hong Kong Backgammon Federation (HKBF)

### Mission
To promote, organise, and govern competitive and social backgammon in Hong Kong.

### Governance Structure

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|---------------|
| **President** | Overall leadership, external relations, fundraising |
| **Vice-President** | Senior figure from HK Backgammon Club — community bridge |
| **League Director** | Manages the inter-club league (scheduling, results, disputes) |
| **Tournament Director** | Organises open tournaments (not club-restricted) |
| **Secretary/Treasurer** | Admin, accounts, communications |
| **Club Representatives** | One captain per club — serves on the committee |

### Activities Beyond the Club League

1. **HK Open Backgammon Championship** — annual open tournament (weekend event, 64+ draw)
2. **Monthly rated tournaments** — in partnership with HK Backgammon Club Meetup
3. **Beginner workshops** — at clubs and public venues to grow the player base
4. **National team selection** — send an HK team to WBGF World Championships (2026 Worlds are in London — perfect timing)
5. **Online league** — modelled on UKBGF National Online League for players who can't attend in person

### Affiliation
- Apply for membership of the [World Backgammon Federation (WBGF)](https://wbgf.info/) as the national federation for Hong Kong
- Adopt UKBGF tournament rules as the basis (well-established, English-language)
- Establish reciprocal relationship with UKBGF (Tony Lezard connection)

### Legal & Financial

- **Registration**: Register as a society under the Hong Kong Societies Ordinance (simple process, ~HK$200)
- **Funding model**:
  - Annual membership fees (individual: HK$200, club: HK$2,000)
  - Tournament entry fees
  - Sponsorship (board game companies, club F&B sponsors)
  - No need for significant capital — backgammon is low-cost to run
- **Insurance**: Public liability via club venues (clubs carry their own)

## Part 4: Key Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|
| Clubs say no | Cast wide net; need only 3 to say yes. Tony Lezard endorsement + London precedent adds credibility. Offer free taster sessions to lower the barrier |
| Not enough players per club | Start with smaller teams (4v4) if needed for Season 1; allow "guest" players in early seasons; run beginner workshops at each club to build squads |
| Small existing backgammon community | This is actually an opportunity — building the scene, not competing with it. Recruit chess/bridge/mahjong players within clubs. The venue quality is the hook |
| Scheduling conflicts | Flexible fixture dates within a monthly window; captains coordinate directly |
| Low awareness of backgammon | Beginner workshops at each club; position as "the new bridge" — strategic, social, time-bounded; leverage the prestige of the club setting to make it aspirational |
| Governance disputes | Clear written rules from day one; adopt proven UKBGF framework |
